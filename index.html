TAO Yuhao | 陶禹皓

I'm a master’s student in Robotics, Systems and Control at ETH Zürich, where I develop robots for in-vivo diagnosis at the Multi-Scale Robotics Lab (MSRL) advised by Professor Bradley Nelson. Previously, I achieved my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering with distinction from RWTH Aachen University.

I am interested in creating body-interfaced devices that sense, interpret, and interact with the human body to improve health and enhance capability. My experience spans in-body medical devices, bioadhesive sensing patches, 3D antenna and embedded systems design, and multi-scale computational mechanics from continuum to atomistic levels.

In my spare time, I cycle through Switzerland's rolling vistas on my Canyon Aeroad and listen to classical music — from Bach to Rach.

Research

Origami-bot

Magnetically navigable, pop-up-fabricated origami capsule robot with an on-board, frequency-reconfigurable 3D electronics component and signal source for channel probing and intestinal monitoring.

Highlights:
  • Structure Design
  • 3D Frequency-reconfigurable Electronics Component Design
  • GHz Frequency Source Design & Integration
  • Pop-Up Fabrication
  • Magnetic Navigation
  • Python + Fortran Magnet Dynamics (Abaqus DLOAD script)

project page / paper

Patch-bot

Magnetic adhesive-patch capsule robot featuring on-board power, Bluetooth communication, and flexible temperature & pH sensors for comfortable and convenient gastric-reflux diagnosis.

Highlights:
  • Adhesive Layer Design
  • Magnetic Layer Design
  • Embedded System Calibration
  • System Integration
  • Magnetic Navigation
  • System In-vivo Test

project page / paper

Droplet Dynamics

Development of a simulation module for sub-Kolmogorov deformable droplets coupled with an in-house Lattice–Boltzmann solver.

Highlights:
  • Rotation-Induced Geometry Error Correction
  • Constant Load Cases
  • Oblate Rising Bubble
  • Droplets in Isotropic Turbulence

project page / bachelor thesis

Atomistic Simulation of Alloy

Atomistic simulation of a medium-entropy alloy with a gradient nano-grained structure under cryogenic-temperature tensile deformation.

Highlights:
  • Voronoi Grain Initialization
  • Hetero-deformation Induced Hardening
  • Grain Rotation Estimation Algorithm

project page / paper / code

Finite Element Simulation of Sintering

Abaqus models with custom UMAT scripts for flash and ultra-high-speed sintering, calibrated against experimental data.

Highlights:
  • UMAT for Grain Growth
  • Temperature Prediction of UHS Sintering
  • Parameter Calibration via Damped-Newton Method

project page

Phase Field Simulation of Sintering

Phase-field study of solid-state sintering that visualises how neighbouring powder grains neck, densify, and rotate—revealing the microstructural physics of porosity loss.

Highlights:
  • Phase-field Modeling
  • Multi-grain Sintering Case
